Mars: The Red Planet

Mars is one of the most interesting planets in our Solar System. It is known as the Red Planet because of its rusty red surface.

Why is Mars important?

Scientists study Mars because it may help us understand whether life could exist beyond Earth. Mars has mountains, valleys, dust storms, and signs that liquid water may have existed there in the past.

Mars Facts

  • Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun.
  • It has two small moons called Phobos and Deimos.
  • A day on Mars is only slightly longer than a day on Earth.
  • Mars has the largest volcano in the Solar System: Olympus Mons.
