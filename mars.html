Mars: The Red Planet

Mars is one of the most interesting planets in our Solar System. It is known as the Red Planet because of its rusty red surface.

Why is Mars important?

Scientists study Mars because it may help us understand whether life could exist beyond Earth.

Mars Facts

  • Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun.
  • It has two moons called Phobos and Deimos.
  • Mars has the largest volcano in the Solar System.
  • Mars is smaller than Earth.
